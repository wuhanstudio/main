Main Features

3D printing is one kind of additive process, where an object is created by laying down successive layers of material. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) technology always end with a visibility of individual layers, in other words, a bad surface quality.

To improve it, you'll need another milling machine or your current machine supports changeable tools. Although some huge industrial manufacturing machines are capable of switching between up to 200 different tools, we want to fit it on a desktop.

High Surface Quality

The hybrid of 3D printing and CNC milling will make it possible to bring your ideas to life smoothly, that is to say fast and with acceptable surface quality.

Reduced Interference

Not only five axis design offers you more flexibility and freedom, alternate 3D printing and milling process can furtherly reduce the occurrence of interference. A surface can be milled before potential interferential structures being printed out.

Automatic Tool Switch

Constant change between extruder and milling tool can be time-consuming and may damage the accuracy with misoperations. We managed to find a way switching tools without manual interference.
